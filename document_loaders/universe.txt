Vast Beginnings
In endless dark, a spark once burned,
And space and time were newly turned.
From dust and fire, stars were born,
Their ancient light still greets each morn.
Galaxies spin in silent grace,
Whirlpools of light through boundless space,
Each star a sun to worlds unknown,
Each world perhaps not truly alone.
We are but stardust, drifting free,
Born of the same cosmic sea,
Atoms once forged in stellar cores
Now walk on earth, on distant shores.
The universe holds no earthly bound,
No edge, no wall, no final ground,
Yet in this vastness, small and true,
We wonder, dream, and reach into the blue.
So look above on a starlit night,
And feel that ancient, borrowed light —
For in each glimmer, far and near,
The universe whispers: you are here.
